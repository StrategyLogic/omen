# Nokia Situation: CEO Appointment Context (2010)

## Situation ID

nokia-elop-2010

## Company Current State

Nokia remains a global handset leader by shipment, but the smartphone transition has accelerated and internal platform execution is fragmented across Symbian and MeeGo programs.

## Appointment Context

A new CEO appointment is expected to reset strategic direction under high market pressure from Apple and Android ecosystem expansion.

## Core Dilemma

Nokia must choose between preserving long-term platform control and achieving short-term market recovery speed.

## Constraint Signals

- Legacy platform stack has high migration inertia.
- Organizational coordination cost is rising across product lines.
- Ecosystem bargaining power is weakening against external platforms.

## Resistance Signals

- Structural conflict between incumbent and transition teams.
- Resource reallocation drag across platform programs.
- Cultural mismatch between historical operating model and new execution cadence.
- Veto-node risk in key governance and partnership decisions.

## Desired Strategic Outcomes

- Restore competitive viability in smartphones.
- Preserve optionality for long-term strategic autonomy.
- Reduce organizational stall during transition.

# Situation Case: SAP looking to pull more external data into its AI platform with Reltio acquisition
- Source URL: https://www.theregister.com/2026/03/30/sap_reltio/
- Capture Date: 2026-04-06
- Event Date: 30 Mar 2026

**Context:** SAP announces acquisition of master data management and data integration specialist Reltio, aiming to integrate external data into its AI platform and boost its Business Data Cloud service.

## The Story
SAP announces its acquisition of Reltio, a master data management firm, with the stated goal of pulling non-SAP data into its AI platform and Business Data Cloud. The move follows SAP's launch of BDC in 2025, a service developed with Databricks to create AI-powered insight apps. However, user group research indicates low familiarity with BDC among members, and analysts note the acquisition represents a shift from BDC's original focus on sharing SAP data externally to now harmonizing external data for use within SAP. A consultant highlights a finding that customers stuck in mid-migration are paying full fees for an unusable system, a line predicted to 'haunt SAP's sales deck for a while.'

### 1. Core Facts (The Facts)
- SAP is to acquire master data management and data integration specialist Reltio.
- The move is positioned to boost adoption of SAP's Business Data Cloud (BDC), launched in February 2025 in alliance with Databricks.
- Reltio uses an AI-based entity resolution system to merge records into a 'golden record' of data about customers, products, suppliers, locations, and employees.
- SAP expects to use Reltio's system to integrate data across SAP and non-SAP applications.
- Reltio migrated its underlying database from Cassandra to Spanner on Google Cloud in 2024.
- Reltio customers include Pfizer, Radisson, and Warner Bros.

### 2. Existing Data and Research Findings (Non-opinion)
- In December, research from DSAG found that 83 percent of its members were only slightly familiar with BDC or not familiar with it at all.
- DSAG found that BDC customers stuck mid-migration end up paying full cloud fees for a system they cannot use.

### 3. Raw Context
- SAP has ERP, HR, CRM, supply chain, and procurement systems.
- SAP has so far struggled to grab users' attention with BDC.
- BDC was set up to build 'insight apps' that use analytics tools and AI models connected to real-time data from applications.

### 4. Direct Quotes (Speaker / Role)
- Muhammad Alam, SAP board exec for product and engineering: "Acquiring [Reltio] will further improve our position as a leading business AI provider, combining SAP and non-SAP data to deliver data context that business AI requires. AI cannot reach its full potential when data is fragmented across business units, platforms and domains without connection or context."
- Tony Baer, principal analyst at dbInsight: "It was a major strategy shift for SAP when it announced BDC a few years back, acknowledging that while SAP is a focal point for enterprise business data, there are other focal points as well. SAP's mission with BDC was first about cleaning its own house with a common 'One Domain Model' for all SAP data. And from that One Domain Model, expose as business products that can be shared with partners like Databricks, Google BigQuery, Snowflake Data Cloud, and Microsoft Fabric."
- Binoy James, SVP applications at SAP consultancy Protera: "That line is going to haunt SAP's sales deck for a while."

### 5. Explicitly Stated Difficulties, Risks & Controversies
- SAP has so far struggled to grab users' attention with BDC.
- 83 percent of DSAG members were only slightly familiar with BDC or not familiar with it at all.

### 6. Unknowns / Open Questions
- Whether the acquisition will successfully boost adoption of SAP's Business Data Cloud.
- How effectively Reltio's system will integrate data across SAP and non-SAP applications for AI agent development.
- The full financial terms of the acquisition are not disclosed.

# Situation: SAP Acquires Reltio to Expand AI Platform with External Data

## Executive Summary

SAP is acquiring Reltio, a master data management (MDM) and data integration specialist, to integrate
non-SAP enterprise data into its AI platform. The strategic move aims to boost adoption of SAP's
Business Data Cloud (BDC) — a service launched in February 2025 in partnership with Databricks.
BDC adoption has been hampered by low user awareness and migration friction, and SAP's leadership
frames Reltio as a key to unlocking cross-enterprise data harmonisation for AI agents.

## Core Strategic Dilemma

SAP launched BDC as a centralised data-sharing layer, but enterprise customers remain rooted in
heterogeneous data estates. The Reltio acquisition signals a strategic pivot: from cleaning SAP's
own data house to actively pulling external data in. The dilemma is whether this moves BDC from
an aspirational product to a deployable enterprise AI data backbone — or whether it compounds
migration complexity for a customer base already stuck mid-transition.

## Key Facts

- SAP is acquiring Reltio to integrate data from outside SAP's application portfolio into its AI platform.
- Reltio provides AI-based entity resolution to produce a unified "golden record" from disparate sources.
- BDC was launched February 2025 with Databricks as strategic partner.
- 83% of DSAG members (German SAP user group) were unfamiliar or only slightly familiar with BDC (Dec 2025 research).
- BDC customers mid-migration pay full cloud fees for a system they cannot yet use.
- Reltio migrated from Cassandra to Spanner (Google Cloud) in 2024, signalling cloud-native alignment.
- Reltio customers include Pfizer, Radisson, and Warner Bros.

## Signals

### S1 — AI Entity Resolution as Integration Moat
Reltio's AI-based entity resolution (golden record) gives SAP a proprietary MDM capability that can
harmonise SAP and non-SAP data. This acts as a structural driver for BDC's relevance to heterogeneous
enterprise data estates.

### S2 — BDC Adoption Gap
DSAG research showing 83% member unfamiliarity is a market constraint. SAP must close an awareness
and trust gap before the Reltio acquisition translates to meaningful BDC pipeline growth.

### S3 — Migration Friction Cost Signal
Customers stuck mid-migration paying for unused cloud infrastructure are a reputation and churn risk.
This constraint dampens near-term BDC expansion velocity and complicates the Reltio onboarding narrative.

### S4 — Databricks Alliance as Amplifier
The pre-existing Databricks partnership creates an amplifier effect: Reltio's MDM layer can extend
the data lake integration story, reducing the "why SAP only" perception among data-first enterprises.

## Strategic Context

- **Core question:** Can SAP transform BDC from a struggling product into a credible enterprise AI data
  backbone by integrating Reltio's MDM capability across heterogeneous data estates?
- **Current state:** BDC exists but suffers from low adoption and painful migration UX. Reltio adds
  cross-enterprise MDM capability.
- **Hard constraints:** Enterprise data gravity, multi-year ERP migration timelines, DSAG trust deficit.
- **Known unknowns:** Whether Reltio integration will compound or simplify migration complexity;
  competitive response from Salesforce Data Cloud and Microsoft Fabric.

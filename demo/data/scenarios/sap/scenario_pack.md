# Scenario Ontology: sap_reltio_acquisition

- pack_id: sap
- pack_version: 1.0.0
- ontology_version: scenario_ontology_v1

## Scenario A: Rule-Changing Offensive: SAP as the AI Data Context Unifier

- goal: Proactively redefine the enterprise AI market by making SAP's platform the central hub for all enterprise data context, not just SAP data.
- target: Market leadership in business AI by capturing the data-context-first shift.
- objective: Integrate Reltio to create a breakthrough, asymmetric advantage in AI-ready data, forcing competitors to play catch-up in external data integration.
- variables:
  - Speed of Reltio integration into BDC (temporal)
  - Customer adoption of new migration model (adoption)
  - External data source connectivity (capability)
- constraints:
  - Must maintain Reltio's cloud-native approach to data integration and MDM
  - Must leverage Reltio's AI-based entity resolution system for golden records
  - Must integrate data from outside SAP's broad application portfolio
- tradeoff_pressure:
  - Pressure to move fast vs. risk of botched integration that reinforces negative customer perceptions.
  - Pressure to offer aggressive migration guarantees vs. protecting near-term revenue from existing BDC contracts.
  - Pressure to open the platform widely vs. protecting SAP's core application ecosystem advantage.
- resistance_assumptions:
  - structural_conflict: 0.7
  - resource_reallocation_drag: 0.6
  - cultural_misalignment: 0.5
  - veto_node_intensity: 0.4
  - aggregate_resistance: 0.55
- assumption_rationale:
  - High structural conflict from internal SAP units protective of their data domains and revenue streams.
  - Significant drag from reallocating engineering and sales resources from legacy BDC to the new integrated vision.
  - Moderate cultural clash between SAP's process-centric culture and Reltio's agile, cloud-native DNA.
- modeling_notes:
  - This scenario bets on SAP's ability to execute a bold, market-making move.
  - Success hinges on overcoming internal inertia and changing the customer conversation from migration risk to AI opportunity.
  - The primary rival is market inertia and customer skepticism, not a specific competitor.

## Scenario B: Defensive Survival: De-risking BDC Adoption and Migration

- goal: Ensure BDC's survival and compliance with hard market constraints by systematically eliminating adoption barriers.
- target: Customer trust and risk reduction.
- objective: Use the Reltio acquisition primarily to address the critical 'pay-during-migration' constraint and rebuild credibility, prioritizing safe adoption over market leadership.
- variables:
  - Migration risk reduction score (risk)
  - Customer familiarity with BDC (awareness)
  - Integration completeness for core SAP apps (technical)
- constraints:
  - Must address customer concerns about paying for unusable systems during migration
  - Must overcome low customer familiarity with BDC (83% of DSAG members)
  - Must integrate data from outside SAP's broad application portfolio
- tradeoff_pressure:
  - Pressure to invest in perfecting migration safety vs. allocating resources to offensive AI feature development.
  - Pressure to simplify messaging to rebuild trust vs. accurately communicating the complex technical advantages of AI-native MDM.
  - Pressure to meet immediate customer demands for safety vs. building the long-term, open data platform vision.
- resistance_assumptions:
  - structural_conflict: 0.3
  - resource_reallocation_drag: 0.8
  - cultural_misalignment: 0.2
  - veto_node_intensity: 0.6
  - aggregate_resistance: 0.48
- assumption_rationale:
  - Lower structural conflict as defensive goal aligns with sales and customer support units feeling pain from BDC failures.
  - Very high resource drag from creating new migration tools, guarantees, and support processes.
  - High veto node intensity from legal and finance teams wary of offering success-based fees and guarantees.
- modeling_notes:
  - This is a compliance and survival scenario. Market leadership is a secondary concern.
  - The primary constraint network is the set of customer objections and market sentiments documented in the signals.
  - Success is measured by stopping the bleeding and stabilizing BDC's reputation.

## Scenario C: Direct Confrontation: Winning the Enterprise Data Hub War

- goal: Compete directly with other data platform vendors (Databricks, Snowflake, Informatica) for the role of central enterprise data hub.
- target: Market share in the enterprise data integration and MDM segment.
- objective: Leverage the combined SAP-Reltio stack to win head-to-head deals against rival platforms, under established rules of feature competition and total cost of ownership.
- variables:
  - Competitive win rate (performance)
  - Feature parity/advantage index (competitive)
  - Total cost of ownership (TCO) advantage (economic)
- constraints:
  - Must integrate data from outside SAP's broad application portfolio
  - Must maintain Reltio's cloud-native approach to data integration and MDM
  - Must leverage Reltio's AI-based entity resolution system for golden records
- tradeoff_pressure:
  - Pressure to match competitor feature velocity vs. maintaining deep integration with the SAP application suite.
  - Pressure to compete on price/TCO vs. protecting the premium value of SAP's enterprise ecosystem.
  - Pressure to sell the combined platform as a standalone product vs. using it primarily as an AI enabler for SAP apps.
- resistance_assumptions:
  - structural_conflict: 0.6
  - resource_reallocation_drag: 0.5
  - cultural_misalignment: 0.4
  - veto_node_intensity: 0.3
  - aggregate_resistance: 0.45
- assumption_rationale:
  - High structural conflict with SAP's traditional sales motion focused on its own applications.
  - Moderate drag from building a dedicated competitive sales and marketing team for the data platform war.
  - Moderate cultural misalignment with Reltio's team now being asked to compete in SAP's large, complex deal cycles.
- modeling_notes:
  - This scenario treats the market as a rule-bounded, resource-competitive arena with clear rivals.
  - The rival network includes other MDM vendors, data lakehouses, and integration platform as a service (iPaaS) players.
  - Success is defined by taking measurable share from specific competitors.
